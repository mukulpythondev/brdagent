Subject: SecurePay SME Lending Portal - rough notes from product, risk, and operations

This is not a clean requirement document. It is a mixed note from a product call, a risk review, and an operations escalation.

Business goal:
We want a portal where small business customers can apply for a working-capital loan, upload KYC documents, connect bank statements, and track application status from submission to approval.

Product team notes:
- Customer should sign in using Google or Microsoft SSO because many SMEs already use these accounts.
- The dashboard should show application status, missing documents, repayment estimate, and next action.
- Customer can upload PAN, GST certificate, bank statement PDF, and owner identity proof.
- If an application is incomplete for more than 48 hours, send email and SMS reminder.
- Relationship managers need an internal review queue with filters for risk score, loan amount, and SLA age.
- Customer should be able to download the sanction letter after approval.

Risk and compliance notes:
- KYC documents must be encrypted at rest.
- Audit logs must keep every status change, reviewer action, and document download event for 7 years.
- Applications above INR 25 lakh require manual risk approval before offer generation.
- The system must flag mismatched applicant name between GST certificate and bank statement.
- PCI-DSS is not directly applicable unless card repayment is added later, but privacy and data-retention controls are mandatory.

Operations notes:
- Current manual process takes 5 to 7 days. Target is under 24 hours for low-risk cases.
- Relationship managers complain that requirements are scattered across email, WhatsApp screenshots, PDFs, and CRM notes.
- Admin team wants CSV/Excel export of applications every day.
- Legal team wants a clear acceptance criterion: no sanction letter should be generated until KYC, bank statement, and risk approval are complete.

Potential conflict:
Security team suggested username and password only because of internal policy, but product wants Google/Microsoft SSO to reduce onboarding friction.

Non-functional expectations:
- System should support 10,000 active SME users in the first quarter.
- 99.9 percent monthly availability.
- Search and filter in internal queue should respond within 2 seconds.
- All sensitive documents must be encrypted at rest and in transit.
- Role-based access is required for customer, relationship manager, risk approver, and admin.

Acceptance examples:
- A customer can submit a complete application with required documents.
- A relationship manager can see the application in the review queue.
- The system blocks sanction letter generation until mandatory checks are passed.
- The BRD must show risks, assumptions, source trace, conflicts, and model routing decisions.
